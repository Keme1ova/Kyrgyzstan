ABOUT NARYN

Naryn Province is the largest province in Kyrgyzstan, occupying the central part of the country. And, paradoxically, it is the most sparsely populated. It is a land of preserved nomadic Kyrgyz life, endless expanses and high mountains.

Almost the entire Naryn region, located in the southeastern part Kyrgyzstan, occupy the mountains. periodically mountain ranges are replaced desert steppes or large forests, intermountain valleys and lakes

History

Historically, the region has always been inhabited by nomadic Kyrgyz, who moved to these places in the xi-xiii centuries, who to this day make up 99% of the population. in naryn like nowhere else preserved nomadic life and way of life, not changing for many centuries - the region has always been relatively remote from wars, trade routes, etc. during the Soviet era, most of the Naryn region was closed to tourism, in order to preserve intact the amazing nature of this region. thanks to this, now the Naryn region is an ideal place for ecotourism and wild travel in the bosom nature. like many other Central Asian cities, Naryn arose like a small fortification on the trade routes that led from Eastern Turkestan to Central Asia, to protect them from robbers. Until the middle of the 19th century, there was no city as such in this area. c accession of Central Asia to the Russian Empire in 1868 here a Russian garrison was placed (Naryn fortification), later Naryn settlement appeared, and from that moment began city ​​construction. through the Naryn fortification with the Seven Rivers the mail service of the consulate of Russia in Kashgarians. after the October Revolution of 1917, Naryn came under control of the red army, but in 1920 there was a clash between the Soviet troops and the White Guards. after the bloody fighting, the city was recaptured, and Soviet power was established here. in 1927 year naryn became the administrative center of the then established Naryn region

Climate

Naryn is the most severe city of the republic: the winter here is long and very cold. climate is sharply continental, arid, average January temperature ranges from -18 to -20C, sometimes it can drop to -38s. summer is warm but very short. precipitation little falls, so the streets are usually dry and dusty. average temperature in July is +20c

Tourism

Naryn attracts not only foreign tourists, but also Kyrgyzstanis who are keen on domestic tourism. nearby are picturesque passes and gorges, mountain rivers and lakes, waterfalls, eternal snows and glaciers. the alpine landscape is also amazing, encircling city. a lot of tourist routes

According to legend, the name of the city is naryn, which means broth from horsemeat. but some sources claim that the word "naryn" came from the Mongolian language and is translated into Russian as "sunny", or from Chinese - "narrow"

Sightseeing

Tash-Rabat

Tash-Rabat - a caravanserai on the territory of the at-Bashi district, naryn region, kyrgyzstan, near the border with china. the building is located on the banks of the winding river tash-rabat, tributary of the Kara-Koyun River, at an altitude of more than 3500 m above sea level. tash-rabat was built in the 15th century on the site of an older Nestorian or Buddhist monastery IX-X century. there are two versions of its foundation: the first - the fortress was founded by the khan, who wanted to protect trade caravans from robbers, and to contribute to the development of the country. second, it was founded local rich man, but the construction of this castle did not end unknown reasons. ever since Central Asia Islam was finally entrenched, the monastery began to decline. however, proximity to the Silk Road did not allow the temple to turn into desert. it was used as a caravanserai. It is believed that tash-rabat was a key point when crossing the Tien Shan, so as not only provided a haven for merchants, but also served fortification during the raids of robbers. through tash-rabat trading caravans were heading to the cities of the Ferghana Valley
